# Trustay Product Spec

## Product name
Trustay

## One-line description
Trustay helps remote workers choose a city area where they can work, train, eat, and stay focused without losing days to research.

## Product thesis
When a remote worker lands in a new city, the hardest part is not finding a pretty place. The hard part is becoming **functional fast**:
- where to stay
- where to work if the stay setup is weak
- where to train
- where to eat well nearby
- which area supports a calm, repeatable routine

Trustay solves that by turning fragmented place data into a simple, confidence-based city setup recommendation.

## User
Primary user:
- remote worker
- stays 7 to 30 days
- values focus, routine, convenience, and physical wellbeing
- wants to avoid wasting the first 1 to 3 days figuring life out

Secondary user:
- founder / freelancer / creator planning a short work trip
- wants quick decision support instead of endless browsing

## Problem statement
Existing tools answer fragments:
- maps show places
- blogs show lifestyle content
- listings show stays
- social threads show opinions

But none of them answer, in one simple decision-oriented flow:
**Where should I base myself in this city so I can work well, train nearby, eat well, and stay organized from day one?**

## Promise
**Land anywhere and get set up faster.**

## Core product outcome
Reduce setup friction and uncertainty for the user's first 7 to 30 days in a new place.

## Phase 1 business model
Revenue-first, simple, not VC-oriented.

Recommended MVP monetization:
- free public city preview
- one-time unlock for deeper city setup details
- Stripe Checkout

## Pricing direction for MVP
- Free: public city page with limited data
- City Pass: unlock full city setup and full place list
- Trustay Pass: unlock multiple cities

Do not build pricing complexity into the app structure yet.
Use environment-driven configuration so pricing can be changed quickly.

## Non-goals
- not a generic nomad community platform
- not a booking engine
- not a review platform at scale
- not a social app
- not a travel inspiration app
- not a surf/hiking-first product

## Product principles
1. Decision support over discovery clutter.
2. Calm, clear UI over travel aesthetics.
3. Honest confidence signals over fake certainty.
4. Simple revenue path over feature breadth.
5. Fast shipping over overengineering.
